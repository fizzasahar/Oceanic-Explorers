Geological Features

Icy Surface

If Hydrothoria is far from its star or lacks sunlight, the surface could be covered in thick ice, with liquid water oceans underneath, similar to moons like Europa (Jupiter's moons) or Enceladus. The icy surface serve as a protective shield for the oceans below.

Ecosystem

Chemosynthesis on Hydrothoria

On Hydrothoria, chemosynthesis is the heartbeat of life,In the absence of sunlight, the organisms of Hydrothoria thrive around hydrothermal vents, turning minerals into energy for survival.Beneath the icy surface of Hydrothoria, chemosynthesis fuels entire ecosystem.

Chemosynthetic organisms utilize various chemical compounds as energy sources, converting chemical energy into organic compounds. Key energy-yielding chemical compounds:

1. Hydrogen sulfide (H2S)
2. Hydrogen gas (H2)
3. Ammonia (NH3)
4. Iron (Fe2+)
5. Manganese (Mn2+)
6. Nitrite (NO2-) and nitrate (NO3-)
7. Sulfur compounds (S, S2-, S0)


Organisms using chemosynthesis:

1. Bacteria

2. Archaea

3. Protozoa

4. Fungi

These microorganisms support life in diverse environments:

1. Hydrothermal vents.

2. Deep-sea sediments.

3. Soil.

4. Freshwater ecosystems.
